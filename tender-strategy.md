## 1. Australian Procurement Landscape

### Federal Government Panels and Standing Offers

#### Digital Marketplace (buy.digital.gov.au) — PRIORITY 1

The Digital Marketplace is the Australian Government's primary platform for procuring digital and ICT services under $1M (and increasingly above that with appropriate approvals). It is operated by the Digital Transformation Agency (DTA).

**How to register as a seller:**

1. Go to buy.digital.gov.au → "Become a seller" → Create an account
2. Choose the appropriate categories to apply for:
   - **Business Analysis** (directly applicable)
   - **Data Science and Management** (covers Power BI, Fabric, analytics)
   - **Strategy and Policy** (covers process design and transformation advisory)
   - **Software Engineering and Development** (if Power Platform / automation work is included)
   - **Emerging Technologies** (AI consulting services)
3. Complete the seller application:
   - ABN and business details
   - Capability statement (500 words per category)
   - At least 2 case studies per category (anonymised clients acceptable for government clients)
   - Evidence of relevant insurance: Professional Indemnity ($2M minimum), Public Liability ($10M minimum)
   - Cyber security requirements: Agree to the ASD Essential 8 baseline or provide evidence of compliance
4. Application is reviewed by DTA. Approval typically takes 2–4 weeks.
5. Once approved, set up your **seller profile** — this is your shop window. Write it for the buyer, not for the algorithm:
   - Headline: "Business Process Intelligence — process mapping, Microsoft Fabric, Power BI"
   - Summary: Lead with outcomes, not methodology
   - Include specific government technology environments you've worked in (SAP, Dynamics 365, Oracle, ServiceNow — even if brief experience)

**Monitoring new opportunities:**
- Check buy.digital.gov.au/opportunities weekly
- Set email alerts for new opportunities using the category filters
- Response window is often 5–10 business days — calendar monitoring is critical

#### AusTender (austender.gov.au) — PRIORITY 1

AusTender is the primary federal government procurement portal for contracts, standing offers, and agency-issued tenders.

**Registration:**
1. Register as a supplier at austender.gov.au → Supplier Registration → Register for procurement notifications
2. DUNS number (now D-U-N-S via DNB) may be required for some agencies — register free at dnb.com.au
3. CAGE code may be required for defence-adjacent contracts — apply via DLA at dla.mil (only pursue if targeting defence agencies)

**RSS feeds to monitor (subscribe all of these):**

- All ATM notices (Approach to Market): `https://www.austender.gov.au/AustenderFeeds/AustenderATMFeed.xml`
- CN notices (Contract Notices, for intelligence on who is winning what): `https://www.austender.gov.au/AustenderFeeds/AustenderCNFeed.xml`
- SON notices (Standing Offer Notices): `https://www.austender.gov.au/AustenderFeeds/AustenderSONFeed.xml`

Add these to an RSS reader (Feedly, Inoreader) and check daily.

**Agency procurement thresholds (Commonwealth Procurement Rules 2023):**
- Under $10K: Direct procurement (no tender required)
- $10K–$80K: May use buy.digital.gov.au or agency-direct request for quote
- $80K–$200K: Must go to market (open tender or from existing panel)
- Over $200K: Full open tender or panel/SON required

Target the $80K–$500K band initially — large enough to be meaningful, small enough that big firms often won't bother.

Several federal agencies maintain standing offer panels for ICT and consulting services. Getting on these panels is a medium-term goal (12–18 months); they require more evidence of past work.

**Key panels to monitor and pursue:**

|---|---|---|---|

## 2. Tender Writing Framework

Every tender response — regardless of agency, scope, or format — follows this structure. Adapt length and depth to the contract value (larger contract = more pages).

---

### Section 1 — Executive Summary (1 page maximum)

**This section wins or loses the bid.** Evaluators often read this and skip to Pricing before reading anything else. It must:
- Name the client's specific problem (use their language, not yours)
- State your proposed approach in 2–3 sentences
- Quantify your capability with one specific, credible statistic
- Close with a single sentence about why ProcessBI specifically is the right choice for *this* work

**Template:**

> [CLIENT NAME] is seeking [their stated objective in their own words]. ProcessBI proposes a [X-phase/X-week] engagement that [outcome statement, not activity statement]. Our principal has delivered [specific comparable work] for [type of organisation similar to theirs], resulting in [measurable outcome]. We combine deep expertise in [their specific technology or domain] with [your differentiator relevant to this tender] to ensure [benefit statement they care about].

Do not open with "ProcessBI is pleased to submit..." — this is throat-clearing. Start with them.

---

### Section 2 — Understanding of Requirements

**Mirror their language exactly.** Use the same terminology, acronyms, and problem framing from the Request for Tender (RFT) document. Evaluators are checking whether you understood the brief.

Structure:
1. Context (2–3 sentences): What we understand about the organisation's current situation
2. The challenge (2–3 sentences): What problem this engagement needs to solve
3. Success criteria: What "done" looks like in their terms
4. What this engagement is not (1 sentence): Sets scope boundaries proactively

**Red flag to avoid:** Do not restate the RFT verbatim. Show that you've synthesised it, added context from public sources (annual reports, audit findings, agency plans), and understand the *real* problem behind the stated problem.

---

### Section 3 — Proposed Approach

ProcessBI's standard phased methodology:

**Phase 1 — Discovery and Current State Assessment** (typically 1–3 weeks)
- Stakeholder interviews (structured, using a pre-built interview guide)
- Process documentation review
- Current-state process mapping (swim lane format)
- Data environment assessment (what data exists, where, in what format)
- Key findings and pain point prioritisation

**Phase 2 — Process Design and Future State** (typically 2–4 weeks)
- Future-state process design (addressing confirmed pain points)
- Gap analysis: current → future
- Process simulation (where volumes justify it)
- Change impact assessment
- Stakeholder review and sign-off

**Phase 3 — Solution Architecture and Build** (varies significantly by scope)
- Data model design
- Microsoft Fabric / Power BI build
- Process automation design and implementation
- Testing and quality assurance

**Phase 4 — Knowledge Transfer and Handover** (typically 1–2 weeks)
- Documentation handover (all artefacts in client-owned formats)
- Staff training
- Post-implementation review
- Hypercare period (typically 2–4 weeks of support)

In the tender, adapt phase names and descriptions to use the client's terminology. If they call it "current state analysis" instead of "discovery" — call it that.

---

### Section 4 — Team Capability

This section must be specific and credible without disclosing identifiable client information.

**Format:**
> **Principal Consultant**: [Do not use a personal name in public tenders — use "our Principal"]. Over [X] years of experience delivering [specific type of work] for [ASX-listed / federal government / APRA-regulated] organisations. Directly relevant experience includes:
> - [Type of engagement], [type of organisation], [outcome achieved]
> - [Technology or method deployed], [scale or complexity indicator]
> [Certifications: Microsoft Certified: Data Engineer Associate, Lean Six Sigma Black Belt, etc.]

If sub-contractors or associates are proposed, include their capability statements in the same anonymised format.

**Certifications to pursue immediately (if not already held):**
- Microsoft Certified: Fabric Analytics Engineer Associate (DP-600)
- Microsoft Certified: Power BI Data Analyst Associate (PL-300)
- Lean Six Sigma Green Belt (minimum) / Black Belt (target)
- Certified Business Analysis Professional (CBAP) — IIBA
- PRINCE2 or PMP (for government tender credibility)

---

### Section 5 — Case Study Evidence

Include 3 case studies, each on a single page. Match case studies to the client's sector where possible.

**Case study format:**

**Background**: [3 sentences: organisation type, size, problem they brought to us]

**Our Approach**: [4–6 bullet points: what we did, how, in what sequence]

**Outcome**: [Quantified where possible: "reduced reporting cycle from 3 days to 4 hours", "identified 12 process redundancies representing $800K in annual administrative cost", "dashboard adoption reached 94% within 60 days of go-live"]

**Quote**: [If client has approved a generic quote, use it. Government clients rarely permit named quotes. Use: "Senior Manager, Federal Government Agency"]

---

### Section 6 — Pricing

**Rate card structure (as of June 2026):**

| Service type | Day rate (AUD, ex-GST) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Principal consulting (advisory, workshops, strategy) | $1,750–$2,250/day | ~$218–$281/hr |
| Process mapping and documentation | $1,500–$1,750/day | Higher volume, lower complexity |
| Power BI / Fabric development | $1,750–$2,000/day | Technical delivery |
| Process automation design | $1,750–$2,000/day | |
| Training and knowledge transfer | $1,500/day | Group sessions |
| Discovery/assessment (fixed price) | $8,000–$15,000 | 2–5 day engagement, fixed scope |

**Fixed-price options:** Always offer a fixed-price alternative for defined-scope phases. Government buyers increasingly prefer fixed-price for anything under 4 weeks. Build in a 15% contingency before presenting the fixed price.

**Payment terms:** 30-day payment terms are standard in government. For engagements over $50K, request milestone-based payment (30% upfront, 40% at Phase 2 completion, 30% at final delivery).

**Risk/reward arrangements:** Rarely appropriate for consulting (hard to attribute outcomes). Only consider for data/analytics work where an efficiency improvement is clearly measurable and attributable.

---

### Section 7 — Why ProcessBI

This section is the differentiator close. Write it *specifically for this tender* — not a generic capability statement.

**Template:**

> ProcessBI brings three specific advantages to [CLIENT NAME]'s [tender name]:
>
> **1. [Differentiator directly relevant to their stated challenge]**: [One sentence of evidence]
>
> **2. [Technical or methodological capability they explicitly need]**: [One sentence of evidence]
>
> **3. [Risk mitigation factor relevant to their concern]**: [One sentence of evidence]
>
> We are the right choice not because we are the largest firm — we are not — but because [the senior person doing the work is the person you've been speaking to / we have delivered exactly this type of engagement for comparable organisations / we are already familiar with your technology environment / we are available to commence within [X] weeks].

## 5. Bid / No-Bid Decision Framework

Score each criterion 1–5. **Bid if total score ≥ 18 out of 25.**

| Criterion | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rate/budget fit** | Budget implies <$150/hr or contract is fixed at below floor | Budget implies $150–$200/hr | Budget clearly supports $200+/hr or is open-market competitive | ×1 |
| **Technical match** | <50% of requirements match our capability | 50–70% match | >70% match — we can answer every requirement with direct experience | ×1 |
| **Relationship advantage** | No prior relationship; cold tender | Know someone peripherally; have read their strategy | Direct relationship with the buyer; they know our work; prior engagement | ×1 |
| **Competition level** | Open market tender, likely 10+ bidders | Panel refresh (limited field); 3–6 bidders | Sole source, limited tender, or we have been specifically requested | ×1 |
| **Timeline and effort ROI** | Effort > 15 days for contract value under $50K | Effort proportionate: 5–10 days for $100K+ | Effort very proportionate: under 5 days for $150K+; or contract likely to repeat/extend | ×1 |

**Scoring guide:**

- **22–25**: Strong bid — prioritise above all other work this week
- **18–21**: Bid — allocate appropriate effort; don't over-invest
- **14–17**: Conditional — only bid if current pipeline is thin; or if there's a specific strategic relationship reason
- **Below 14**: No bid — write a polite decline or simply don't submit

**Mandatory no-bid conditions (override any score):**
- Rate floor not met (implied rate below $150/hr)
- Submission deadline is fewer than 5 business days away and contract value is under $200K

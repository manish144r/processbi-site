### We Don't Just Build Your Data Solution — We Help You Fund It

Investing in Microsoft Fabric or Power BI is one of the highest-ROI decisions a modern business can make. But many of our clients are surprised to discover that government grants can cover a significant portion of the cost.

At ProcessBI, identifying applicable funding programs is a standard part of every engagement. We surface the opportunities, build the documentation, and structure our project deliverables to maximise eligible expenditure — so you get more value from every dollar invested.

---

#### What Grants Are Available?

Australia has more than a dozen active grant programs that may apply to a data transformation project. The most relevant include:

**R&D Tax Incentive**  
If your Fabric implementation involves technical innovation — custom data models, novel integration approaches, or AI/ML analytics — you may be eligible for a 43.5% refundable tax offset (SMEs under $20M revenue). This is one of the most valuable and underutilised incentives available to Australian tech projects.

**Entrepreneurs' Programme – Business Growth Services**  
Up to $100,000 in matched funding for SMEs to adopt technology and build capability. ProcessBI consulting fees, Microsoft licences, and training costs are all eligible expenditure. Available to most industry sectors.

**AI Adopt Program**  
Up to $250,000 for SMEs adopting AI technologies. Fabric with Microsoft Copilot, Power BI AI visuals, or Azure ML integration qualifies as genuine AI adoption.

**State Manufacturing Grants (NSW, VIC, QLD)**  
Manufacturers can access state-level grants of $100,000–$1,000,000 to fund digital manufacturing intelligence platforms — exactly what ProcessBI delivers.

**Technology Vouchers**  
Smaller businesses can access $5,000–$50,000 in state government technology vouchers to fund initial Power BI or Fabric advisory engagements.

#### How ProcessBI Supports Your Grant

We're not a grants agency — but we make grant applications significantly easier and more successful by providing:

- **Grant Opportunity Brief** — a ranked list of applicable programs for your specific industry, state, size, and project scope, delivered at the start of every engagement
- **Technical project description** — written in the language grant programs require, covering methodology, technical challenges, and innovation aspects
- **Outcome projections** — quantified benefits (reporting time saved, cost reduction, data quality improvement) in the format required by most programs
- **Budget schedule** — structured to ensure ProcessBI fees, Microsoft licences, and training costs are clearly documented as eligible expenditure
- **Application support** — we partner with registered grants consultants to prepare complete applications for larger programs

---

#### Who Qualifies?

Most of our clients qualify for at least one program. You're likely eligible if:

✓ You're an Australian business with an ABN  
✓ You have at least 5 employees  
✓ You're investing in Microsoft Fabric, Power BI, or a related data platform  
✓ Your revenue is under $20M (for the highest-value SME rates)  
✓ You're in NSW, VIC, or QLD (additional state programs apply)

Manufacturing businesses with 10–500 employees and revenue of $1M–$100M are typically in the strongest position, but we've identified applicable grants for businesses across retail, professional services, logistics, and healthcare as well.

---

#### A Real Example

A NSW-based manufacturer with 45 staff and $8M revenue engaged ProcessBI to build a Fabric data warehouse consolidating ERP and production data. Our grant scan identified two applicable programs:

- **NSW Manufacturing Modernisation Fund** — $160,000 at 50% co-contribution (covering half the project cost)
- **R&D Tax Incentive** — 43.5% refundable offset on the R&D component, estimated $22,000 refund

Total effective grant coverage: **~$182,000 against a $210,000 project** — a net client cost of approximately $28,000 for a solution that delivers $150,000+ per year in reporting savings.

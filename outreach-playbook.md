## 1. LinkedIn Outreach Sequences

### Ground Rules (Read Before Using Any Sequence)

- **Never send a template verbatim.** Every message must have at least one personalised sentence — their specific company, a recent post they made, a news story about their industry, or a specific credential relevant to them. Buyers smell templates instantly.
- **Respect the LinkedIn ecosystem.** These are professional relationships, not leads to harvest. If someone declines or doesn't respond after Message 4, do not contact again for at least 6 months.
- **Rate limit:** Maximum 15–20 new connection requests per day. LinkedIn flags accounts that fire hundreds of requests in a short period.
- **Track everything.** Maintain a simple CRM (even a spreadsheet) with: name, company, title, connection date, message 1 sent date, message 2 date, message 3 date, outcome.
- **Timing:** Send messages Tuesday–Thursday, 7:30–9:00 AM or 5:00–6:30 PM AEST. These windows have highest open rates.

**Message 4 — Breakup message (send 14 days after Message 3, if no response)**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],

**Why this works:** The breakup message reliably generates replies. People who've been meaning to respond and haven't often reply to this one. Leaving on a positive note keeps the door open — enterprise sales cycles can be 12–18 months.

## 2. Target Account List Framework

### Priority Account Criteria

An account qualifies as a priority target if it meets ALL of:

**Size:** 500+ employees in Australia (or 200+ in a high-maturity sector like financial services or federal government)

**Sector:** Financial services (APRA-regulated: banks, insurers, super funds, credit unions), federal or state government, utilities and energy, manufacturing, telecommunications

**Technology signal:** Microsoft-first environment evident from LinkedIn job postings, press releases, or known enterprise agreements (Azure, M365, Dynamics 365, Power Platform)

**Buying signal (at least one of):**
- Announced a cloud migration or ERP upgrade in the past 18 months
- New CIO, CDO, or COO hired in the past 12 months (first 100 days = highest buying propensity)
- Posted a job for a Business Analyst or Power BI developer (signals internal resource gap)
- Received an audit finding related to process controls or data governance (APRA, ANAO, state audit offices)
- Announced a cost reduction or operational efficiency program
- Published a digital strategy or data strategy document recently

**Rate signal:** Budget for external consulting is evident from public procurement records (AusTender/state portals) or known spend with large firms (implies willingness to pay market rates)

## 3. Cold Email Templates

Use email only when LinkedIn is not available or has been exhausted. Work email addresses can be found via professional directories, company websites, or tools like Hunter.io or Apollo.io (if subscribed).

**Format rules:**
- Subject line under 50 characters
- Body: 4 lines maximum (mobile-first — most are read on phones)
- CTA: one clear action only
- PS: always include — it's the second most-read part of an email

## 4. Content-Led Outreach

The most effective outreach hook is a piece of content that the prospect would genuinely find valuable — not a brochure, but an insight that makes them think. This approach works because:
1. It gives you a legitimate reason to reach out ("I wrote something relevant to your situation")
2. The prospect sees your thinking before they see your pitch — you demonstrate capability before asking for anything
3. Content that resonates gets forwarded internally (the best lead you can get)

**The Formula:**

> "I wrote something about [TOPIC THEY CARE ABOUT] because I kept seeing [PATTERN IN THEIR SECTOR]. Given [THEIR SPECIFIC SITUATION — recent announcement / audit / hiring activity], thought it might be relevant. Happy to share the full piece if useful."

This works better as a LinkedIn message than email because the prospect can click through to your profile to validate who you are.

## 5. Partnership Strategy

### Microsoft Partner Network

**Why this matters:** Microsoft Fabric and Power BI are the core of ProcessBI's technology stack. Being a Microsoft Partner signals credibility to procurement teams, gives access to Microsoft-referred leads, and qualifies you for co-sell arrangements.

**How to join:**
1. Register at partner.microsoft.com → Create an account using your business email
2. Enrol in the **Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program** (formerly MPN)
3. Work toward **Solutions Partner** designation in **Data & AI (Azure)** — requires:
   - Customer success (net new customer deployments of Fabric/Power BI)
   - Performance (Azure consumed revenue from client environments)
   - Skilling (certified employees — DP-600 and PL-300 count)
4. Alternative path: Register as an **Independent Software Vendor (ISV)** if you develop any Power BI solutions or Fabric apps

**Benefits:** Listed in Microsoft's partner directory (discoverable by enterprise clients looking for Fabric/Power BI consultants), eligible for Microsoft co-sell, access to Microsoft FAST track engineers for complex technical scenarios, NFR software licenses.

**Target in Year 1:** At minimum, achieve **Action Pack** membership (AUD ~$450/year) for basic partner benefits and Microsoft's logo use rights.

---

### Institute of Analytics Professionals of Australia (IAPA)

- Member: iapa.org.au → Join as a Practitioner or Professional member
- Benefits: Credibility with analytics-literate clients (CDOs, data leads); conference speaking opportunities; network of analytics professionals who may refer work; access to the Analytics Leaders Community
- Get involved: Volunteer for a chapter event, submit a presentation, join a community working group
- Cost: ~AUD $350–$650/year depending on membership tier

---

### BPMI / Object Management Group (BPM Standards)

- BPMI is absorbed into the Object Management Group (OMG) — the body that maintains BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) standards
- Membership is less important than BPMN certification as a credential
- Pursue: **OMG Certified Expert in BPM (OCEB 2)** or equivalent — signals deep process methodology credentials to enterprise buyers
- Note: The **Association of Business Process Management Professionals (ABPMP)** is the practitioner body — join ABPMP International and look for the Australian chapter (abpmp.org)

---

### Lean Six Sigma Training Bodies — Referral Partnerships

The following training providers certify hundreds of Australian professionals in Lean/Six Sigma each year. Graduates often need implementation support — this is a referral source.

- **Lean Six Sigma Experts (LSS Experts Australia)** — approach about a referral arrangement for clients needing implementation consulting after certification
- **Velopi / Simplicite** — similar approach
- **Australian Institute of Management (AIM)** — runs Lean and process management programs; potential referral agreement

**Pitch for referral agreement:**
> "Your graduates leave with certification. Some of them then need help embedding the methodology in their organisations. We'd be glad to be the firm you recommend for implementation — no fee arrangement required initially, just a reciprocal referral relationship."

Engagement FAQ

Common Questions

How is an engagement scoped?
Scoping starts with a free 30-minute discovery call. We ask about the business problem, the processes involved, the number of stakeholders and process domains, and any technical constraints. From that conversation we produce a written scope within one business day — covering deliverables, timeline, milestone structure, and any assumptions. The scope is fixed: we don't charge for work that wasn't in the scope. If your requirements expand, we agree a change scope before proceeding.
Do you offer retainers?
Yes. Retainer arrangements are available for clients who need ongoing process, BI, and AI advisory beyond a defined project. Retainer scope is structured quarterly — we agree what's included, set a minimum commitment per cycle, and review at each quarter. Retainer clients get priority scheduling and first access to new service offerings (such as agentic AI integration work). Contact us to discuss whether a retainer structure makes sense for your situation.
What's included in a Process Discovery workshop?
A Process Discovery workshop typically includes: a pre-workshop stakeholder briefing pack, facilitated current-state process walkthrough sessions (typically half-day per process domain), SIPOC analysis, swim lane documentation in BPMN 2.0, a gap and waste analysis report, a root-cause classification matrix, a prioritised improvement roadmap, and an executive summary. All diagrams are delivered in editable format (Visio-compatible and PDF). The workshop itself is run remotely or on-site — whichever works for your team.
Can we combine engagement types?
Absolutely — and most clients do. A common path is Process Discovery → Process Transformation → Fabric & BI Implementation, treating each as a phase of a continuous engagement. We can scope them as separate projects with independent gate reviews, or as a single end-to-end programme of work. Multi-phase programmes typically benefit from a reduced total scope estimate compared to commissioning each phase separately. We'll advise on the best structure during the initial discovery call.
Do you work with government procurement frameworks?
Yes. We have delivered engagements through NSW Government procurement frameworks and can work within standard government contracting requirements including milestone-based payment schedules, deliverable acceptance criteria, and audit trail documentation. If your agency requires panel registration or a specific contract form, let us know during the discovery call and we'll advise on fit.
